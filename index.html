"We Wish You..."

Chinese Provincial Flag Creation

There’s a lot more to that colorful array of flags on the wall than meets the eye. After researching the geography, history, culture and economy in detail, I took it upon myself to create flags for Chinese provinces. My goal was to design flags that are simple, yet effective– “good flags”. Flags not only serve as symbols of various political divisions: They can inspire nationalism– love for one’s country and state, and are often treated with the same degree of respect as the thing they represent. When flown, they are public affirmations of loyalty and pride. A flag is clearly far more than a piece of fabric. So, check out my final product! I’ll be around; Feel free to ask questions (wouldn’t you like to know what makes a flag good or bad?)

  • Led By: Benjamin Maltz

Something Sweet

We will be making traditional Chinese New Year Desserts such as 汤圆 (tāng yuán), 年糕 (nián gāo), and 蛋挞 (dàn tǎ). Tāng Yuán consists of sweet “dumplings” in a sweet soup usually eaten on the 15th day of the celebration known as Yuanxiao, or the Lantern Festival. Nián Gāo, also known as Year Cake, is a type of sticky rice cake usually eaten during the Duanwu Festival and is considered lucky to have it during this time. Dàn Tǎ is an egg custard tart with a pastry crust and egg filling, which can be found in Hong Kong, Portugal, England, and multiple other asian countries. So, if you would like to try to make something sweet and good to eat come on over to learn more about this fascinating part of the Chinese New Year!
